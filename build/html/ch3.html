Corporate Risk¶

Idk what this example illustrates¶

sell price = 5
cost = 3
margin = 2 or 40%
FC = 5000
\[\begin{split}VC = Variable Costs \\ FC = Fixed Costs \\ Sales - VC - FC = EBITDA (Breakeven)\end{split}\]

Degree Of Operating Leverage (DOL)¶

  • imagine two companies with same product, price point, etc.etc., except only that they have different cost structures

    • could amount to different risk

    • e.g. better machinery, less workers vs. worse machienery, more workers

      • machinery -> FC, workers -> VC
\[DOL = \frac {Percent Change In EBITDA} {Percent Change in Sales}\]

Derivation of DOL

\[ \begin{align}\begin{aligned}\begin{split}EBITDA &= Sales - VC - FC (1) \\ \\\end{split}\\\begin{split}if Sales \uparrow 1 \% then \\\end{split}\\\begin{split}EBITDA* &= 1.01 * Sales - 1.01 * VC - FC (2) \\ \Delta EBITDA &= 0.01 * Sales - 0.01 * VC (2 - 1) \\ \% \Delta EBITDA &= \frac {\Delta EBITDA} {EBITDA} \\ \\ &= \frac {0.01 * Sales - 0.01 * VC} {Sales - VC - FC} \\ \\\end{split}\\\begin{split}and \\\end{split}\\\begin{split}DOL &= \frac {\frac {0.01 Sales - 0.01 VC} {Sales - VC - FC}} {0.01} \\ \\ &= \frac {Sales - VC} {Sales - VC - FC}\end{split}\end{aligned}\end{align} \]

Contirbution Margin¶

\[\begin{split}S - VC = Units Sold * \frac {Contribution Margin (CM)} {Unit} \\ \\ DOL = \frac {Unit Sales * \frac {CM} {Unit}} {Unit Sales * \frac {CM} {Unit} - FC}\end{split}\]

also

\[\begin{split}S - VC = Sales * \frac {Contribution Margin (CM)} {$} \\ \\ DOL = \frac {Sales * \frac {CM} {$}} {Sales * \frac {CM} {$} - FC}\end{split}\]

How DOL Affects EBITDA¶

  • when fixed costs are high, risk is high

    • when times are bad, FC owns you
    • when times are good, you get a lot of $$ b/c of low VC

Example¶

Capital Intensive Labour Intensive
Sales 10,000 10,000
VC % 20% 60%
VC 2000 6000
FC 6000 2000
EBITDA 2000 2000
Sales \(\uparrow\) 10% 11000 11000
VC 2200 6600
FC 6000 2000
EBITDA 2800 2400
DOL \(\frac {0.8 * 1000} {0.8*1000-6000} = 4\) \(\frac {0.4 * 1000} {0.4*1000-2000} = 2\)
